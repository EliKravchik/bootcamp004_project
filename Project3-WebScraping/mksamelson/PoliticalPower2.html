Introduction

The implementation of federal law in the United States is predicated on passing votes in the Congress, the Senate, with final approval by the President.

Key considerations for the success of a bill is necessarily based on the characteristics of the Congress and Senate.

Congressional and Senate ideological leanings can have a distinct impact on whether or not a bill becomes law.

Objectives

  2. Understand the data characteristics
